Legal Metrology Packaged Commodities (LMPC)

The Law Metrology Act of 2009 governs the import, sale, and distribution of prepackaged goods in India. Prepackaged goods are those packed in advance with a predetermined quantity, not in the presence of the buyer. Registering products under this Act reflects a commitment to consumer awareness and helps combat unfair trade practices.

Importing pre-packaged goods mandates LMPC (Legal Metrology Packaged Commodities) registration. According to the Legal Metrology Packaged Commodities Rules of 2011, importers and packers of pre-packaged products must register under Rule 27 of the Legal Metrology Act of 2009. Applications should be submitted within 90 days of commencing import operations.

To obtain an LMPC license, it is crucial to handle goods accurately and genuinely, ensuring correct measurements. It's important to note that LMPC certification is not required for packers importing goods weighing 10 grams/milliliters or less or agricultural products exceeding 50 kilograms in weight.

Why there is a need of LMPC?

Before importing pre-packaged goods for sale or distribution in the country, importers and manufacturers must obtain an LMPC (Legal Metrology Packaged Commodity) importer license. This requirement is mandated under Rule 27 of the Legal Metrology Packaged Commodity Rules.

For those involved in selling pre-packaged commodities within a single state, the application for the LMPC license should be directed to the Controller of Legal Metrology in that specific state, as stipulated by the Department of Consumer Affairs.

In the case of importers selling products in multiple states or local manufacturers distributing their products across several states, the same rules apply, and registration should be sought from the Director at the national level. This ensures compliance with regulations for the sale of pre-packaged goods across different regions.
